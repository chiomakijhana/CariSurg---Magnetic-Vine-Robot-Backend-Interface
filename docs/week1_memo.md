## 2. Background

Minimally invasive surgery (MIS) has become the preferred approach for a growing range of diagnostic and therapeutic procedures, offering reduced patient trauma, shorter recovery times, and lower rates of post-operative complication compared to open surgery. However, conventional MIS instruments are constrained by their rigidity: they follow fixed insertion trajectories and offer limited manoeuvrability within body cavities, particularly in anatomically complex or obstructed regions.

The **Magnetic Vine Robot (MVR)** is a prototype soft robotic system under development. It draws inspiration from the growth mechanics of climbing vine plants; extending, steering, and retracting through tissue via magnetic actuation rather than mechanical linkages. This approach enables single-entry navigation to target sites that would otherwise require multiple incisions or open access, making it particularly suited to **tissue biopsy** and **small lesion removal** in minimally invasive contexts.

## 3. Clinical Problem

- **Access constraints** — rigid instruments cannot reach lesions behind anatomical structures without additional incisions

## 5. Literature Summary

Five papers were reviewed this week covering soft robotics in surgery, magnetic actuation systems, AI-assisted MIS, and intraoperative decision support. Key findings:

- Tip-Growing Robots: Design, Theory, Application (Harthy et al., 2024) – Provides a comprehensive overview of tip-growing robot design principles, theoretical foundations, and applications in navigating confined and complex environments.
- Real-time Force/Position Control of Soft Growing Robots: A Data-Driven Model Predictive Approach (Kalibala et al.) – Demonstrates a data-driven model predictive control framework that enables accurate real-time force and position control of soft growing robots.
- Magnetic Vine Robot Platform – Presents a magnetic steering mechanism that improves maneuverability and directional control of vine robots in constrained spaces.
- Branching Vine Robot Study – Introduces a branching architecture that allows a single vine robot to access multiple pathways, improving workspace coverage at the expense of increased system complexity.
- Additive Manufacturing for Vine Robots – Explores the use of additive manufacturing to produce customized, lightweight, and integrated vine robot components with reduced fabrication complexity.

## 6. Gaps Identified

- Tip-Growing Robots: Design, Theory, Application (Harthy et al., 2024) – Existing tip-growing robots face challenges with payload capacity, precision control, and clinical translation.
- Real-time Force/Position Control of Soft Growing Robots (Kalibala et al.) – The control framework requires significant computational resources and has limited validation in complex real-world environments.
- Magnetic Vine Robot Platform – Dependence on external magnetic systems may limit portability and increase backend complexity.
- Branching Vine Robot Study – Branching improves reach but introduces greater mechanical complexity and control challenges.
- Additive Manufacturing for Vine Robots – Manufacturing advances have not fully addressed the integration of compact actuation, sensing, and control systems.
